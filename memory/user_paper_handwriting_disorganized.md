Justin describes himself as "very disorganized with paper and handwriting in the real world." His personal motivation for building the wearer-measurements feature *now* was wanting a digital notebook for his son's pants project — not because measurements are a drafting prerequisite, but because the slip of paper is the thing he loses.

**How to apply:**
- When proposing copy or framing for the Measurements feature, lean into the "digital notebook beats lost paper" positioning rather than service-to-drafting framing.
- When evaluating features that could replace paper habits (notes on patterns, sketch-to-digital tooling, label printing) — Justin will probably value them. Surface them as ideas worth considering.
- Don't conflate "Justin built this" with "everyone needs this." The wearer feature exists for organizational convenience; he's the first user but won't be the last. Position it as opt-in for the user, not as the path.
